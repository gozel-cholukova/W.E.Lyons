BYRON-BETHANY IRRIGATION DISTRICT
BYRON, CALIFORNIA
The Byron-Bethany Irrigation District serves a 100 square mile area of Eastern Contra Costa County providing municipal, industrial, and agricultural water to over 150 customers. W.E. Lyons Construction assembled a proven design-build team to deliver the new 35,000 square foot facility on a single site reducing overall operating costs and increasing efficiency. The new administration building provides modern offices, future expansion areas, and a large public boardroom with state of the art audio/visual system for enhanced training and hosting of regional events.

BANNEKER HOMES
SAN FRANCISCO, CALIFORNIA
W.E. Lyons Construction developed a comprehensive plan to provide the highest possible cost benefit for upgrading this 108-unit low income housing project. We completed the interior and exterior renovations to all seven buildings, completely renovated a 40,000 square foot concrete parking garage and successfully performed the design-build of a new freestanding community building in the existing courtyard. The entire project was completed within a condensed time frame (12 months).

LLNL CAFETERIA
LIVERMORE, CALIFORNIA
Through the design-build and value engineering process W.E. Lyons Construction helped both Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory and their food service subcontractor arrive at an equitable footprint; balancing total cost with total square footage. Along with our design-build partners we achieved the client’s request to provide an inviting and exciting dining experience, obtaining the highest quality project within the assigned budget. The 17,000 square foot cafeteria boasts 30 foot clear-story with maximized day lighting for reduced energy consumption.

CCWD MAINTENANCE BUILDING
CONCORD, CALIFORNIA
The Contra Costa Water District Maintenance Building projects in Concord and Antioch consisted of two separate 20,000 square foot design-build facilities, 40 miles apart. The scope of the project included high-bay shop facilities, conventional offices, conference rooms, break rooms, locker rooms, and showers. Using the design-build project delivery approach, the entire project was completed in less than 12 months. The team encouraged open communication and a focus on user group meetings to achieve desired goals.

GREG LYONS
LEED AP, President

As President of W.E. Lyons Construction, Greg Lyons provides oversight and contributes to the development of strategy and talent of the company. He helps manage overall corporate performance, and contributes his financial and business expertise that has been developed over a 30-year career, including more than 25 years at Lyons serving as President, project manager, and estimator. He assists in business development with an emphasis on renovation and serves as a project sponsor on all projects. HISTORY
BALANCING TRADITION WITH INNOVATION

W.E. Lyons Construction has been in business since 1926. Owned and managed by the Lyons family for three generations, the company survived the Great Depression and a world war by providing its customers with excellent service and quality construction at a reasonable cost.

Because our family name is also our business name, our personal reputation depends on the success of every project. At W.E. Lyons Construction, we put our clients' needs first and each of our projects receives the personal attention of one of the company's principals. This personal attention sets us apart and aids in the overall success of each project.

In addition to our personal commitment, our size offers certain benefits. As a medium-size firm, our lower overhead and relatively flat organizational structure makes us an affordable and flexible alternative to the contracting giants.

W.E. Lyons Construction has successfully integrated 21st Century technology, innovative building materials, computer-assisted project management and design into our daily tasks. However, we have never forgotten the old-fashioned customer service that has been our mainstay over 80 years.
